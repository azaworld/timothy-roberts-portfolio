executive profile

The Story

In 1984, at age 14, I started out on Commodore, Apple, and DOS machines — drawn to video games, telecommunications, programming, and bulletin board systems. While other kids played games, I was online over 300bps modems, living through the evolution from BBSs and ARPANET into what we now call the Internet.

That early fascination became a 25+ year run building and scaling platforms: I co-founded SAVVIS (the first high-QoS national backbone, later acquired by CenturyLink for ~$3.4B), and took Phantom Entertainment, StationDigital, and HashingSpace public. Today I'm CEO of Platformz and COO/CTO of FUR4 — inventor and principal designer of a multi-tenant commerce platform. Here's the journey:

family & legacy

A St. Louis family of builders

Timothy Munro Roberts was born in St. Louis in 1970, into a prominent family rooted in the city since the early 1800s — a lineage of lawyers, marketers, and entrepreneurs who each, in their own field, built something lasting.

⚖️

Munro Roberts Sr.

Great-grandfather

Nationally known labor lawyer

A labor-union organizer turned lawyer who became Director of Labor Relations and aide-de-camp to publisher Joseph Pulitzer at the St. Louis Post-Dispatch. He founded the typographers' union.

🏛️

Munro Roberts Jr.

Grandfather

Prominent St. Louis attorney · 1947–1992

After serving in WWII, he founded the firm now known as Roberts, Perryman & Bonkamp. He represented or sat on the boards of Laclede Gas, Southwestern Bell, the St. Louis Globe-Democrat, Samuel I. Newhouse, and the ABC Television Network.

🏁

William “Monty” Munro Roberts III

Father

Marketing innovator — Ford · Anheuser-Busch · Y&R

A Ford Foundation Scholar at Yale and Washington University graduate. As Ford's Director of Marketing he helped create the Shelby and Mercury divisions and ran Ford Racing; designed Busch Gardens attractions (Tampa's 'Dark Continent' and the Loch Ness Monster coaster); helped launch Busch beer; and originated NASCAR's Busch Series, the Busch Clash, and the 17-year 'Head for the Mountains' campaign. He later led the Walt Disney account at Y&R before becoming an ordained Episcopalian minister.

💡

Lora Roberts

Mother

Serial entrepreneur

An acclaimed businesswoman who has started and owned a business brokerage, franchise auto-brake stores, and a chemical-distribution company — and today owns MedHire, a neuroscience recruitment firm.

St. Louis-based and a proud father of four. His startups have created over 4,000 high-paying jobs. After Hurricane Katrina he managed operations for a Red Cross disaster center in Atlanta, helping 400+ victims alongside 20+ agencies. Off the clock: offshore fishing (3rd place, 2015 Billfish Memorial Day Shootout), ice hockey, golf, tennis, and strategy games.

enterprise software philosophy

Why most enterprise software fails

Most enterprise software fails because every department buys a different system.

  • ✕Marketing buys one platform.
  • ✕Finance buys another.
  • ✕Operations buys another.
  • ✕Customer service buys another.

Soon the business owns dozens of disconnected applications — requiring expensive integrations, duplicate data entry, inconsistent reporting, and endless manual processes.

Timothy believes this approach is fundamentally broken. Every business should operate through a unified architecture — not a pile of disconnected tools.

🗄️

Common data model

One shared model of the business, so every application speaks the same language.

🔐

Common security framework

One security and permissions layer across every function, not dozens of silos.

⚡

Common automation engine

One engine that automates work across the whole organization.

  • 📊Measurable
  • ⚙️Automated

Questions & answers

Who is Timothy Munro Roberts?+

Timothy Munro Roberts (Tim Roberts) is a technology founder, enterprise architect, and platform designer with 25+ years building and scaling commerce and infrastructure platforms. He is currently CEO of Platformz and COO & CTO of FUR4, and was born in St. Louis, Missouri in 1970.

Did Tim Roberts found SAVVIS?+

Yes. In 1994 he co-founded Diamond.net (aka SAVVIS Communications) and served as CTO, designing the first high-quality-of-service national Internet backbone. SAVVIS went public (NASDAQ: SVVS) and was later acquired by CenturyLink (now Lumen) for approximately $3.4B.

What companies has Tim Roberts founded or led?+

SAVVIS (co-founder & CTO), Intira (founder & CEO), Phoenix Networks (director & investor), Phantom Entertainment (founder, CEO & CTO), GameStreamer (CEO & CTO), Savtira (founder, chairman & CEO), StationDigital (founder & CEO), HashingSpace (founder & CEO), CEOSavvy (managing director), and today Platformz (CEO) and FUR4 (COO & CTO).

What is Tim Roberts known for in cloud computing?+

He founded Intira in 1997 — the first company ever to sell cloud computing as a service ('Netsourcing'). Intira's lineage continued through Data Return and Terremark (NASDAQ: TMRK), which Verizon acquired for approximately $1.6B.

How many companies has Tim Roberts taken public?+

Four: SAVVIS (NASDAQ IPO), and Phantom Entertainment, StationDigital, and HashingSpace via reverse mergers.

How many patents does Tim Roberts hold?+

He is an inventor on 5 patents from Savtira's cloud-commerce platform (application streaming, encoding, and digital-goods auto-publishing), with additional filings prepared for the Platformz multi-tenant architecture.

What is Platformz?+

Platformz is a multi-tenant, enterprise-class commerce platform and agency (100% owned by FUR4) that powers consumer brands. It's built on React/Next, Magento 2, Strapi, GraphQL, AWS, and Cloudflare, with tenantized portals for customers, dealers, referrals, and more.

Where is Tim Roberts based?+

St. Louis, Missouri, USA.
